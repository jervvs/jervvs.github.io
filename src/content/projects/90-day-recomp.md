A 9-12 month project to lose 20kg at ~0.5-0.75kg/week. The approach: **diet drives the weight loss, training protects the muscle.**

Built around my actual constraints — a shoulder injury (no overhead pressing), flat feet (low-impact only), and a schedule that needs to work around shift work.

## The System

The insight that made this click: treat body recomposition like an engineering problem. Define the constraints, set the targets, build a system that's simple enough to follow without thinking.

**Nutrition:** ~2,100-2,300 kcal/day, ~150-170g protein. A "low-decision system" — protein first every meal, then vegetables, then a controlled carb. Pick 2-3 default meals and rotate. Track for 3-4 weeks to learn portions, then eyeball.

**Training:** 6 days a week, posterior-chain and core focused. Two strength sessions (dumbbells only), one cycle, one swim, one core session, one long cardio. Daily mobility habit at 8:15am — joint circles, calf raises, balance work.

**Sleep:** Anchored midnight-8am. Wind-down at 11:15pm. Non-negotiable.

## The Rules That Hold It Together

1. Follow the day, don't negotiate
2. Something beats nothing — half a session counts
3. Miss a day? Skip it, don't double up
4. Weigh weekly, same morning, ignore daily noise
5. Protein every meal, sleep the priority

The plan is designed to run for 6-8 weeks before making any changes. Consistency is the point, not optimisation.
