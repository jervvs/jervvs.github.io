The best dashboards aren't the ones with the most data. They're the ones that show you **exactly what you need to know** and nothing else.

I'm designing a dashboard that answers three questions at a glance:
1. **Is anything broken right now?**
2. **Is anything trending toward broken?**
3. **What changed recently?**

If the answer to all three is "nothing," the dashboard should feel calm — mostly empty, mostly green. It should only get visually busy when something actually needs attention.

The design principle: **information density should scale with urgency.** A healthy system gets a quiet dashboard. A system in trouble gets a loud one.

This is harder than it sounds. Most dashboard tools default to "show everything" — every metric, every chart, every label. The discipline is in what you *leave out*.
