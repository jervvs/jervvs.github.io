I treat my own life a little like I treat production systems: figure out what actually matters, build the systems that support it, measure, and iterate. This group collects that work in the open — the research, the tooling, and the practice — so it's useful to anyone doing the same.

It's the same instinct behind [A Better Me](/building/better-me/), taken a layer deeper: from *intention* to an actual operating system.

## What's here

- **[The Concept Map of a Successful and Happy Life](/writing/living/concept-map-happy-life/)** — the *why*. A survey of the self-help canon against the science of well-being: ~70 concepts across ten clusters, with the popular versions honestly weighed against the evidence. The single most robust finding — close relationships outrank money, fame, and IQ for long-term happiness — anchors everything else.
- **[Compound — Design Doc](/writing/living/compound-design-doc/)** and **[Phase 0 Build Spec](/writing/living/compound-build-spec/)** — the *system*. A personal life-OS whose job is to do the administrative thinking of life (what's on my plate, what's the plan, did I do it) so my attention is freed for the part that matters — being present. Built on the research above, deliberately designed to hand agency back, never to absorb it.
- **[Complete Training Manual](/writing/living/training-manual/)** — the *practice*. A concrete, evidence-led training plan (body control, fitness, fat loss) that operationalizes the physical-wellbeing lever the research keeps pointing at.

The through-line: **automate the logistics of living; never automate the experiencing of it.**
