I'm studying toward five different engineering roles at once — and doing it in the open. This section is the map: the honest gap analyses, the roadmaps I'm working through, and the method I use to cut one CV into a role-specific version without lying about any of it.

The whole thing rests on one idea: **maintain a single evidence bank — a superset of everything I've genuinely done — and tailor a cut of it per application.** Not five separate stories; one truth, framed five ways.

## How this fits together

- **[The 2026 ATS Playbook](/writing/career/ats-playbook-2026/)** — the verified rules for getting a technical CV past both legacy keyword screeners and newer AI/LLM ones. Read this once; most "beat-the-bot" advice is myth.
- **[One Master CV, Five Roles](/writing/career/tailoring-one-cv-five-roles/)** — the tailoring method itself: how the evidence bank becomes a role-specific résumé.
- **A learning plan per role** — for each target, where I'm genuinely strong, where the honest gaps are, and the concrete roadmap (with proof artifacts) to close them.

Where I'd bet first: **data/production** and **AI-for-SWE** — the two tracks where I already win on evidence, not just potential.

Every plan here is candid about what I *haven't* done. That's deliberate. The point of doing this in public isn't to look finished — it's to show the work of closing a gap, so the roadmap is useful to anyone else walking a similar path.
