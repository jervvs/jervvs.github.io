I used to think writing was something writers did. Professionals with bylines and book deals. Not engineers with terminals and on-call rotations.

I was wrong. Writing is **thinking made visible.** When you write about something, you discover what you actually understand and what you've been hand-waving. The gaps become obvious. The connections become clear.

This is why I'm building a writing practice — not to become a writer, but to become a **clearer thinker.**

## What I'm Learning

- **Frequency matters more than quality.** A mediocre post published is worth more than a perfect post stuck in drafts. The quality comes from the reps.
- **Writing for an audience changes the thinking.** When you write for yourself, you can be lazy. When you write for others, you have to earn their attention.
- **The hardest part is starting.** Once the first paragraph exists, the rest follows. The blank page is the real enemy.

## Why It's in "Building"

Because a writing practice isn't a project you finish. It's a muscle you develop. Some weeks I write a lot; some weeks I write nothing. The point isn't perfection — it's the long arc of showing up.
