This isn't a New Year's resolution. It's an ongoing, open-ended project to get healthier, stronger, and more intentional about how I take care of myself.

The approach: **treat self-improvement like engineering.** Define constraints, build systems, measure progress, iterate.

## What This Looks Like Right Now

**Physical:** I'm in the middle of a [90-day recomposition project](/projects/90-day-recomp/) — losing fat while preserving muscle, built around a structured nutrition and training plan. The system is designed for consistency, not heroics. Low-decision meals, low-impact training (I have a shoulder injury), sleep anchored to a fixed schedule.

**Mental:** Reading more deliberately. Writing more regularly (that's its own building item — [A Writing Practice](/building/writing-habit/)). Trying to spend less time consuming and more time creating.

**Habits:** The insight that changed my thinking: **you don't rise to the level of your goals, you fall to the level of your systems.** (James Clear said it better than I could.) So instead of setting ambitious goals, I'm building small, sustainable defaults:
- Daily mobility at 8:15am
- Protein first at every meal
- Wind-down at 11:15pm
- Write something every week
